Preparation of Green Tea

Ingredients :

  • 1 teaspoon of loose green tea leaves or 1 green tea bag
  • Water (about 8 ounces or 240 ml)
  • Optional: Honey, lemon, or mint for flavor

Preparation :

  1. Boil Water :

    • Start by bringing water to a boil. It's important to use fresh, cold water for the best flavor. If you're using a kettle, wait for it to whistle, and then let it sit for a moment to cool slightly.

  2. Preheat Teapot or Teacup :

    • Pour a small amount of the hot water into the teapot or teacup to preheat it. Swirl the hot water around, then discard it.

  3. Add Green Tea Leaves or Tea Bag :

    • Place the green tea leaves or tea bag into the preheated teapot or teacup.

  4. Pour Hot Water Ove the Tea :

    • Pour the hot water over the tea leaves or tea bag. Ensure that the water is hot but not boiling to preserve the delicate flavors of the green tea.

  5. Steeping time :

    • Let the tea steep for about 2-3 minutes. Steeping time can vary depending on personal preference. If you like a stronger flavor, you can steep it for a bit longer, but be cautious not to over-steep, as it can lead to a bitter taste.

  6. Remove Tea Leaves or Tea Bag :

    • Once the tea has steeped to your liking, remove the tea leaves or tea bag. If you're using loose tea leaves, you may want to use a strainer to filter them out.

  7. Optional Add Flavorings :

    • If desired, you can add honey, lemon, or mint for extra flavor. Adjust the sweetness and acidity to your preference.

  8. Enjoy :

    • Your green tea is now ready to be enjoyed! Sip it slowly and savor the subtle and refreshing taste.

Remember that green tea is delicate, so it's best to avoid using water that is too hot or steeping it for too long to prevent bitterness. Experiment with the steeping time and optional flavorings to find the perfect balance for your taste.
